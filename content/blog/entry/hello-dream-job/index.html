It's been two months since I posted Passionate Developer seeks Dream Job, and I've been overwhelmed by the positive response it has generated - lots of well wishes, kind words, advice, and several really interesting opportunities. Even better, I'm happy to announce that the story has a happy ending.

The short version is that I start with Mozilla next week, working as a contractor with the Services team. It's been quite a ride to get here, so read on if you're interested in the long-version debrief.

{% endmark %}

Perhaps the most surprising thing is that there are lots of opportunities out there that would tick many, if not most, of the boxes in my list. I've spoken to big open-source companies like Mozilla and Canonical, independent open-source vendors like flowplayer and nexB, local companies like Citrus and Kogan, and of course my fair share of recruiters.

If you're a python developer with big dreams and a passion for open-source, do yourself a favour and check out some of the links above. The opportunities are definitely out there.

Over the past few weeks I have had the privilege to interview with two of the world's leading open-source technology companies. I spoke to nine great developers from around the world.

So is Mozilla my dream job? Let's take a look: I want to be challenged. I want to hack with world-class developers and grow to be one myself. I want the opportunity to learn and to teach every day. I want to be part of a team that loves their code and loves their job. Yep. "Super geniuses". Love what they do. I want a job that I can take with me and do from anywhere. Or at least anywhere with broadband. One in France, one in Toronto, a bunch throghout the US. I want to write code that matters. Mozilla are helping to build the future of the internet. I think that qualifies. I want a company that respects open source. Everything they do is open. I want flexibility. I want to start late if we've had a rough night with a child. I want to start early if there's an idea I can't get out of my head. Once in a while I want to cram the work week into four days and spend a long weekend doing something with the family. And I want confidence that not only will I get the job done, but I'll get it done well. Hire smart people, let them do their thing. I want a company that would accept a github page as a resume and a blog post as a cover letter. And as long as my family isn't left wanting, I can give my kids the opportunities they deserve, and I don't feel under-appreciated, then all of these things are more important to me than the figure on the paycheck. I'm not naturally adventurous, took a lot of post that initial article. But so glad I did. Now I had a lot to prove, but I'm excited for the challenge.
